About Me

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Economics at Rice University, Houston, Texas. My research in development economics focuses on poverty, culture, refugees, religion, and conflict - mostly centered around the African continent.

My work has involved estimating individual poverty rates, analyzing children's malnutrition rates, and identifying important ethnic characteristics. In doing so, I have worked with several datasets, including household surveys, current and historical ethnic data, the Afrobarometer, and the Demographic and Health Survey.

If you want to know more, feel free to contact me.
